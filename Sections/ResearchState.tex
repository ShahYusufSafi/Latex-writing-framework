\section{State of Research}

The piezoresistive effect has been extensively studied \cite{smith1954,doll2013} in experimental and engineering contexts, 
particularly in sensor design and microelectromechanical systems (MEMS). Classical works established the fundamental 
behavior of strain-dependent conductivity in semiconductors, while more recent research focuses on device optimization 
and fabrication techniques.

However, most existing approaches treat the electromechanical coupling in an application-specific manner. 
From a mathematical perspective, coupled PDE systems with state-dependent coefficients are well understood in general, 
but their systematic application to piezoresistive materials remains limited. This motivates the development of a 
unified computational framework capable of capturing the underlying multiphysics behavior.

Based on these considerations, the proposed study is guided by the following research questions:

\begin{itemize}
    \item How can strain-dependent conductivity be incorporated into a consistent continuum PDE model?
    \item How does mechanical deformation influence the distribution of electrical potential?
    \item What are the effects of nonlinear coupling on system behavior and sensitivity?
    \item To what extent can data-driven approaches improve the modeling of $\sigma(\varepsilon)$?
\end{itemize}

To address these questions, the research will be structured around the following key components:

\begin{itemize}
    \item Introduction and physical background
    \item Derivation of the coupled electromechanical model
    \item Analysis of the nonlinear PDE system
    \item Computational methods and numerical experiments
    \item Results and discussion
\end{itemize}
